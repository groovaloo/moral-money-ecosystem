Moral Money – Digital Constitution

This document defines the fundamental principles of the Moral Money community system. It functions as the constitutional layer of the ecosystem and establishes the ethical and institutional rules that guide all other components of the system.

Core Principle

Power and reputation within the system must emerge exclusively from real contributions to the survival and prosperity of the community.

Contribution may take multiple forms, including work, capital, knowledge, infrastructure, and services. No form of contribution is inherently superior. What matters is the real impact of the contribution on the community.

Ethical Equivalence

Different types of contribution must be treated as ethically equivalent when they produce comparable benefits for the system. Financial investment that enables infrastructure and productive capacity is considered as valuable as direct labor contribution.

Merit-Based Authority

Authority in the system does not come from wealth, status, or ownership. Authority emerges dynamically from demonstrated competence and consistent contribution within specific domains.

Protocol Integrity

The blockchain protocol represents the source of truth for the system. Critical records, decisions, and state changes must be recorded on-chain. Artificial intelligence systems may assist analysis but cannot override protocol rules.

Governance by Competence

Decisions affecting a specific domain must be taken by individuals who demonstrate the highest competence and reputation in that domain.

Transparency and Verifiability

All relevant system actions, including project participation and governance decisions, must remain transparent and verifiable through the protocol.

Evolution of the Constitution

This constitution may evolve over time. However, changes require a formal governance process and strong consensus within the community to preserve system stability.
