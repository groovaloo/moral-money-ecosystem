Moral Money – Reputation System

The reputation system measures the real contribution of each participant to the community.

Reputation is domain-specific and grows through successful participation in projects.

Each participant has separate reputation scores for different domains such as construction, agriculture, energy, governance, health and logistics.

Reputation gained in one domain does not automatically transfer to another domain. This ensures that expertise is recognized only where it actually exists.

Reputation grows through successful project participation, reliability and consistent contribution. Positive feedback from collaborators and participation in high-impact projects can accelerate reputation growth.

Reputation growth is not linear. High-impact projects may produce larger reputation gains than routine activities.
