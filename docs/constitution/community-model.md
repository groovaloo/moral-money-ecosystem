# Community Model

This document defines how the constitutional principles of the Moral Money ecosystem are translated into the operational structure of the community.

## Projects as the Core Unit

All productive activity in the community is organized as projects.

Projects may include:

- Building infrastructure
- Agricultural production
- Maintenance work
- Technical development
- Knowledge sharing

Projects may be small or large. The system must support both.

## Participation

Members may join projects voluntarily.

Participation is recorded and later used to evaluate contribution and reputation.

## Project Lifecycle

Each project follows a basic lifecycle:

1. Creation
2. Participation
3. Execution
4. Completion
5. Evaluation

Completion may be confirmed collectively by participants.

## Domain Structure

Community activity is divided into domains.

Examples of domains include:

- Construction
- Agriculture
- Energy
- Logistics
- Health
- Governance

Reputation is calculated separately within each domain.

## Conflict Resolution

When participants disagree about the outcome of a project, the system selects a temporary council.

The council consists of five members with the highest reputation in the relevant domain.

This council evaluates the situation and produces a decision.

After the decision, the council dissolves.
