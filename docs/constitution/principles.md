# Constitutional Principles

This document translates the constitutional foundation of the Moral Money ecosystem into operational principles that guide the design of the system.

## 1. Contribution-Based Authority

Authority inside the community must emerge from real contributions that improve the survival, resilience and prosperity of the collective.

Authority cannot be purchased.

## 2. Ethical Equivalence of Contribution

Different forms of contribution may have equal ethical value.

Examples:

- A worker contributing daily labor.
- An investor financing infrastructure.
- A specialist providing critical knowledge.

The system must recognize these contributions without privileging one category by default.

## 3. Transparency of Collective Impact

All relevant community actions must be recorded in a transparent system so that the collective impact of contributions can be evaluated.

This is one of the primary reasons for using a blockchain infrastructure.

## 4. Algorithmic Neutrality

Algorithms used to evaluate contributions must act as neutral evaluators.

They must reduce manipulation and minimize the influence of social power structures.

## 5. Institutional Stability

The constitutional layer of the system must change rarely and only through strong collective agreement.

Lower layers of the system may evolve more rapidly.
