# Moral Money Constitution

The Moral Money ecosystem is governed by a constitutional layer that defines the ethical and institutional foundation of the system.

All governance rules, algorithms, economic mechanisms and blockchain implementations must remain consistent with this constitution.

## Core Principle

Power and authority inside the system must emerge from real contributions to the survival, resilience and prosperity of the community.

## Forms of Contribution

Contribution may take different forms, including but not limited to:

- Work
- Capital
- Knowledge
- Infrastructure
- Services

No form of contribution is inherently superior.

What matters is the measurable impact on the well-being and development of the community.

## Ethical Equivalence

Different types of contribution may have equivalent ethical value.

For example:

A worker contributing daily labor and an investor financing critical infrastructure may both contribute to the survival and prosperity of the community.

The system must recognize this equivalence.

## Constitutional Authority

The constitution represents the highest normative layer of the Moral Money ecosystem.

All technical implementations must respect the constitutional framework defined in this document.

Changes to the constitution must be rare and require strong collective agreement.
