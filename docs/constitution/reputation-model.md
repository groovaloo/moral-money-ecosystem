# Reputation Model

This document defines how contribution is translated into reputation inside the Moral Money ecosystem.

Reputation is the primary mechanism through which authority emerges in the community.

## Domain-Based Reputation

Reputation is domain specific.

Examples:

- Construction reputation
- Agriculture reputation
- Governance reputation
- Logistics reputation
- Energy reputation

Reputation in one domain does not automatically transfer to another.

This ensures that expertise is recognized only where it is demonstrated.

## Reputation Growth

Reputation grows through successful participation in projects.

Important factors include:

- reliability
- consistency
- technical competence
- successful completion of tasks
- contribution to high-impact projects

High-impact projects may produce significantly larger reputation gains.

## Reputation Loss

Reputation may decrease when:

- contributions repeatedly cause problems
- projects require significant rework
- participants act in ways that damage the community

The system must focus on long-term contribution patterns rather than isolated mistakes.

## Reputation and Governance

Reputation is used to select temporary governance councils.

When a decision is required in a domain, the system selects the five participants with the highest reputation in that domain.

These participants form a temporary decision council.

After the decision, the council dissolves.

## Reputation Transparency

All reputation changes must be recorded transparently so that the community can understand how authority emerges within the system.
