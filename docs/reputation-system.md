Moral Money – Reputation System

Overview
The reputation system measures the real contribution of each participant to the community.
Reputation is domain-specific and grows through successful participation in projects.

Domain-Based Reputation

Each participant has separate reputation scores for different domains.

Examples of domains:

construction
agriculture
energy
governance
health
logistics

Reputation gained in one domain does not automatically transfer to another domain.

This ensures that expertise is recognized where it actually exists.

Reputation Growth

Reputation grows through successful participation in projects.

Key factors that increase reputation include:

- successful completion of tasks
- reliability and consistency
- positive feedback from collaborators
- contribution to high-impact projects

Reputation growth should not be linear.

High-impact projects may generate significantly larger reputation gains than routine activities.


Reputation Decay

Reputation should slowly decrease when a participant becomes inactive.

The purpose of reputation decay is to ensure that governance and influence remain connected to active participation in the community.

Decay should be gradual and predictable.

Possible decay rules may include:

- small periodic reductions in inactive domains
- slower decay for historically high contributors
- no decay for very recent activity

This mechanism prevents permanent accumulation of influence without continued contribution.


Reputation and Governance

Reputation directly influences governance participation.

When a decision is required in a specific domain, the system automatically selects the participants with the highest reputation in that domain.

The top five individuals form a temporary decision council.

This council reviews the case and makes a decision.

After the decision is made, the council dissolves automatically.

This mechanism ensures that authority emerges from demonstrated competence rather than permanent political roles.


Anti-Manipulation Safeguards

The system must include protections against coordinated manipulation of reputation.

Possible safeguards include:

- weighting evaluations based on evaluator reputation
- detecting repeated mutual rating patterns between specific users
- reducing the influence of tightly connected rating clusters
- flagging abnormal reputation growth for review

These mechanisms help ensure that reputation reflects genuine contribution rather than coordinated manipulation.
